Why Cooking Food??

Food needs to be processed if it is to meet human nutritional needs or practical needs for storage and transportation. Cooking is the main way of food processing. In the modern age when nutritional needs are gradually being met, cooking begins to pursue color, fragrance and taste.

The expansion of agriculture, commerce, trade and transportation between different regions and civilizations provided chefs with many new inventions and techniques, which in turn gave rise to different cuisines. For example French cuisine, Italian cuisine and Chinese cuisine. More and more convenient conditions and a variety of food materials have also attracted many people to love and try cooking.

This webpage is going to provide some cooking knowledge and recipes of Italian food, and also provide a simple assessment to test whether the visitor has acquired the appropriate knowledge of Italian cuisine, and then give out the feedback.

Italian Foods Development and features

History

The Italian cuisine can claim traceable roots as far back as the 4th century BC, Roman Empire. It is said that the first Italian cuisine originated in Sicily. Then, influenced by different neighboring countries or Arab trade, different seasonal ingredients and spices and herbs were added to the dishes of different regions. This has also led to Italian cuisine being known for its diversity.

Features

Italian cuisine is famous for its fresh ingredients, delicate seasonings and spices. In northern Italy, polenta, risotto and pasta are same popular. In central Italy, tomato sauce and meat sauce are traditionally served with fish and pasta. Southern Italy, olive oil, sardines and other ingredients are an important part of the cuisine. In addition, pasta in Italy is diverse and widely spread around the world, such as macaroni and pizza.
