Pasta

Origins

Pasta is an italian delicacy, that has been at the forefront of Italian culture for many generations. There is conjecture as to whether pasta’s (noodles) origins began in China & Italy. After reading over Lin-Liu’s discoveries in On the Noodle Road the question is still open ended. What we do know however is that pasta would have been hard to make in Chinese conditions. China was more exposed to fine wheat, whereas coarse wheat is more prevalent in the creation of pasta. In around 1000BC was the first-time pasta was discovered in the Mediterranean region, which involves around the combination of water and flour to create dough. From there, pasta has evolved as the Mediterranean regions expanded and split off, with it being most heavily linked to Italian food culture. Pasta is such a versatile ingredient, as you can have so many different types, including making spaghetti, ravioli, and lasagna just to name a few. Below will be recipe for sausage pasta, a staple of Italian cooking.

Recipe

Ingredients

3 tbspextra virgin olive oil
1onions, diced
3garlic cloves
400g tintinned Tomatoes
700g jarpassatta
500 g/or one packetspaghetti pasta
500 g or 1cacciatore sausage
150g kalamata olives
100 gspinach
1 knobbutter
3 tbsp grated parmesan cheese
 salt & pepper

Method

1. Heat a medium-sized saucepan over medium–high heat. Add a generous splash of extra virgin olive oil and the onion and cook for 3–4 minutes.

2. Add the garlic and cook for 1–2 minutes, until fragrant

3. Grill cacciatore sausage and cook for 3–4 minutes cooked through, ass to onions & garlic.

4. Add the tinned tomato's, olives & passatta and cook off for 15 minutes.

5. Add butter with salt & pepper to taste & bring to boil.

6. Add spaghetti pasta to boiling water and stir until the water has returned to the boil. Reduce the heat, cover and cook the pasta at a fast simmer for 6 minutes or until pasta is cooked through.

7. Mix pasta & sauce well and serve with parmesan cheese atop & with basil/parsley if at your disposal.
