Arancini

Origins

The history of arancini as well as many other typical Sicilian meals still remain veiled in mystery & folk tales. The first documentation of arancini's existence dates back to the 19th century. Surprisingly, there has been an evolution to arancini's, as they first started off as more of a sweet food rather than savoury. This included using staple italian ingredients such as ricotta, sugar & milk. The introduction of tomato made the switch from sweet to savoury, with ragu being paired to create a very well known italian entree.

Recipe

Ingredients

300 g (3 cups)dried breadcrumbs
250 gmozzarella
60 g (0.5 cups)peas
6eggs
2onion
2 clovegarlic
400 g Tinned diced tomatoes
700mlpassatta
250 gpork Mince
250 gveal mince
250ml (1 cups)vegetable stock
1 bunchbasil
800 g (4 cups)rice, preferably long grain
25 gparmesan

Method

1. Cook off onion & garlic off with oil for 3 minutes (onion before garlic).

2. Add tomatoes, vegetable stock (1/2 that prescribed) & basil (season with salt & pepper). Let simmer & stir for around 1 hour.

3. cook rice, let it sit until cooled and proceed to add the mixture above along with parmesan cheese.

4. Cook off another onion & garlic, but this time add the two mince mixes until browned.

5. To this new mix, add passatta, stock (other half) and basil & bring to the boil. Let this sit for an hour and season with salt and pepper.

6. Disperse the breadcrumbs on a tray and divide the rice mixture
