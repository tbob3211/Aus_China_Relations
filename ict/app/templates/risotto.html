Risotto

Origins

Risotto is another Italian cuisine that shares a very similar base to that of many Chinese cuisines, that being rice. Predominately, the Roman empire relied on rice for medicinal purposes in contrast to an actual food source. It took the Arab settlement in the 10th century to really establish the farming of rice within Mediterranean land to see a rise in the consumption of rice. However, it took till the 15th century until risotto was starting to be produced. Risotto refers to boiling rice within broth (this can be meat based or vegetable based). In evolution, we have seen the addition of eggs, wine and butter used to enhance the flavour of risotto. There are specific types of rice that work best when making risotto, including arborio and carnaroli. SBS pasta history/origins

Recipe

Ingredients

2 Litres (or 8 cups)chicken stock
300 g (or 1.5 cups)arborio rice
250 gporchini mushrooms
1 onion
1 bunchrocket
100 gbutter
80 g (1 cup)parmesan cheese

Method

1. Pour chicken stock into pot and bring to the simmer.

2. Cook mushrooms with half the butter prescribed in saucepan on medium heat, this should take around 5-7 minutes.

3. Cook off onions for around 3 minutes.

4. Add the arborio rice & cook until the rice appears translucent.

5. Add stock 1 cup at a time, making sure to add further cups as the stock gets absorbed by the rice & onions, this process takes around 15 to 20 minutes.

6. After all stock is used, take off the heat and add the mushrooms back, along with rocket, parmesan and the rest of the butter.
