Pasta

Origins

Pasta is an italian delicacy, that has been at the forefront of Italian culture for many generations. There is conjecture as to whether pasta’s (noodles) origins began in China & Italy. After reading over Lin-Liu’s discoveries in ‘On the Noodle Road’ the question is still open ended. What we do know however is that pasta would have been hard to make in Chinese conditions. China was more exposed to fine wheat, whereas coarse wheat is more prevalent in the creation of pasta. In around 1000BC was the first-time pasta was discovered in the Mediterranean region, which involves around the combination of water and flour to create dough. From there, pasta has evolved as the Mediterranean regions expanded and split off, with it being most heavily linked to Italian food culture. Pasta is such a versatile ingredient, as you can have so many different types, including making spaghetti, ravioli, and lasagna just to name a few.
